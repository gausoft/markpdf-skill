# Executive Summary

Acme posted **$4.2M in revenue** in Q3, up **18% QoQ** and **62% YoY**. Net revenue retention reached **118%**, the highest since the company's founding. The leadership team recommends approving the proposed Q4 hiring plan and the European expansion budget.

## Key Highlights

- Revenue: **$4.2M** (+18% QoQ, +62% YoY)
- Active customers: **1,847** (+11% QoQ)
- Gross margin: **78.4%** (+1.2pp QoQ)
- Net revenue retention: **118%**
- Churn: **2.1%** (-0.4pp QoQ)
- Cash runway: **22 months** at current burn

# Revenue Performance

## By Segment

| Segment | Q2 2025 | Q3 2025 | Δ QoQ | Mix |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Enterprise | $2.1M | $2.6M | +24% | 62% |
| Mid-market | $1.0M | $1.1M | +10% | 26% |
| SMB | $0.5M | $0.5M | +0% | 12% |
| **Total** | **$3.6M** | **$4.2M** | **+18%** | **100%** |

Enterprise growth was driven by three logo wins above $200K ARR each, including the largest contract in company history ($420K).

## By Geography

North America remains the dominant market at **71%** of revenue. EMEA grew fastest at **+42% QoQ** off a small base, validating the planned European expansion.

# Customer Health

> "Acme has become essential to our procurement workflow. We can't imagine going back."
> — VP Operations, Fortune 500 retailer

Net Promoter Score increased to **62** (+8 points QoQ). Top drivers cited in survey responses:

1. Product reliability (mentioned by 78% of promoters)
2. Customer support responsiveness (64%)
3. Integration breadth (51%)

# Q4 Recommendations

The leadership team recommends three priorities:

1. **Approve EMEA expansion budget** — $1.8M for office, hiring, localization
2. **Accelerate enterprise hiring** — +6 AEs, +3 SEs in North America
3. **Launch tier-2 pricing** — capture mid-market segment more aggressively
